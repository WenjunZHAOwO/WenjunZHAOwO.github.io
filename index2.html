About Me

Hi, this is Wenjun Zhao. I am currently a second year PhD student of Center of Atmosphere and Ocean Science, Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences at New York University since 2016 September.

My first name 'Wenjun' is pronounced as 'when june'. In Chinese, my name implies a well educated and gentle girl. Enrolled at the age of 17 in School of the Gifted Young (SCGY), I earned a bachelor degree in mathematics at University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). I also worked as a visiting student in the Department of Computer Science at University of Oxford.

My research interest lies in statistical and numerical methods in atmosphere ocean science.

Academic Experiences

Model Verification for Partially Observable Stochastic Hybrid System
(2015 July--September, at University of Oxford)
Supervisor: Prof.Alessandro Abate
Based on the point-based algorithm developed by Dr. Kendra Lesser, I wrote a code in MATLAB and Java to generate a discrete model to do model checking of Partially Observable Markov Decision Process (POMDP). Then I also created code to check similar things for multi-objective system. Some case study of dynamic systems have been tested out. A GUI is also designed and will be available online in the future.
For more details, click here to read my draft of report.

  

A Wavelet-based Image Retrieval System
(2015 June, Class Project of Wavelet Analysis at USTC)
Supervisor: Prof.Juyong Zhang
The similarity of images can be assessed by the characteristics of their wavelet coefficients. In this project, I made abstractions of 1000 given pictures by getting their wavelet coefficients and defining a feature vector based on these matrices with Matlab. The I analyzed their distances and plotted the Precision-Recall (PR ) curve of the results. Furthermore, I also managed to test the stability when the images were rotated or cut into pieces.
From this project, I had a better understanding of how the wavelet analysis works in application and got familiar with the wavelet toolbox in Matlab.

The Mathematical Contest in Modeling: Macro Scheduling in Face of Ebola
(2015 February)
I led a team with two other girls in the four-day contest this winter. Running a clear and efficient labor division, we set up models about spreading trends of disease, transportation methods and allocation of medicine and vaccinates in Africa with MATLAB, Mathematica and C.
I was responsible for the allocation system and paper writing. Through this experience, I was able to apply my knowledge into reality and experienced the satisfaction of research for the first time. To read our paper, click here. Finally we were awarded as Meritorious Winner.

   

Numerical Method Solving the Resistance of Grid Problems
(2013 June, Project Contest of Electromagnetics)
Our team introduced a new method in circuit analysis and programmed using MATLAB and Mathematica to analyze and solve the equations. At last we gave solutions to a series of resistance problems about finite and infinite grids under various circumstances. We set up equations about some classical finite examples, such as tetrahedron, octahedron and C60. As for infinite cases, we use fourier analysis to do calculation and exploit about the property of the solution.
